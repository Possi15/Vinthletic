About Triathlon

A triathlon is a multisport endurance event consisting of three continuous and sequential disciplines: swimming, cycling, and running.

Disciplines:

  • Swimming: The first discipline involves swimming a specified distance in open water (such as a lake, river, or ocean) or in a pool.
  • Cycling: Following the swim, participants quickly transition to cycling, where they ride a bicycle over a predetermined course.
  • Running: Finally, participants transition from cycling to running, completing the event with a footrace over various terrains, such as roads, trails, or tracks.

Distances:

The distances for each discipline can vary depending on the specific type of triathlon. Common triathlon distances include:

  • Sprint: 750m swim, 20km bike, 5km run
  • Olympic: 1.5km swim, 40km bike, 10km run
  • Half Ironman: 1.9km swim, 90km bike, 21.1km run
  • Ironman: 3.8km swim, 180km bike, 42.2km run

History:

The modern triathlon originated in the 1970s and has since grown in popularity worldwide. The first official Ironman Triathlon was held in Hawaii in 1978, consisting of a 2.4-mile swim, 112-mile bike ride, and 26.2-mile run.
